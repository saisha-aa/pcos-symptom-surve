What is PCOS?

Polycystic Ovary Syndrome (PCOS) is a hormonal disorder that causes irregular periods, acne, weight gain, excess hair growth, and other symptoms. It's incredibly common - affecting about 10% of women - but most people with PCOS don't know they have it.

Why? Because these symptoms are constantly dismissed as "part of being a teenager" or blamed on not trying hard enough. By the time most people get diagnosed, they've been dealing with symptoms for years.

My Story

I created this tool after being diagnosed with PCOS. But the diagnosis wasn't a problem - it was an answer. Symptoms I had dismissed for years were suddenly connected with a possible solution in sight.

I soon realized many women I knew had the same diagnosis. Or worse, they had symptoms but no diagnosis at all. PCOS is severely underresearched, and most people who have it don't even know.

If you've been told your irregular periods are just stress, your acne is a hygiene issue, or your fatigue means you're lazy - this is for you.

What This Tool Does

This isn't a diagnosis. It's a validation tool - a way to recognize patterns in your symptoms and get the language you need to advocate for yourself with doctors.

You'll answer questions about symptoms you might not have connected to PCOS, learn what's actually happening in your body, and get specific scripts for talking to healthcare providers who might dismiss you.
